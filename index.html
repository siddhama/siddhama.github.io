ABOUT

Draped around the great Himalayan mountains, with the scenic resplendency of mother nature, windswept peaks, encompassed by many holy rivers and adventure all around, We, the MTB (Mountain Biking club) at IIT Mandi welcome you all to be a part of this... journey!

MTB club is an embark upon a peregrination in the lap of the Himalayas with the most spectacular destination with astounding trails which is a crown jewel in itself for IIT Mandi.

Riding from the finest road to the astonishing Himalayan forest, fascinated by the snow-capped mountains and many more adventure throughout its way, it's a great opportunity for all of us to relish the glory of nature differently.

MTB club is leading its role since 2017. The club is a community of ebullient riders, inclusive of students, faculty and staff. Riding for us is not just a sport or hobby, it's a way of life. Many riders have joined us from time to time in our rides and able to develop their self- reliance skills, endurance, and optimum fitness. We aim to introduce the world to the wondrous experience of riding in the Himalayas.

From the novice to the foremost advanced rider, everyone can have the two-wheel fun for twenty-four hours and explore some native trails.

We at MTB Club aim to provide you the thrill of experiencing riding in the mountains. To fulfil this aim, apart from regular rides organised by the club, you can rent the bikes at nominal rates. You must fill the form, and deposit a refundable security.

The early spring of 2017 saw a dawn of a new culture in IIT Mandi when a few faculty members, staff and students purchased personal mountain bikes for leisure and sport activities. Hardly a dozen in number, they started to venture out to early morning rides in nearby villages and mountain tops. Having had exposure of a wonderful experience of riding up and down... the mountain slopes, sometimes off road too, everyone saw the worth of their investment in the semi-premium Mountain Bikes.

In the academic year 2017-18, Mountain Biking Club, abbreviated as MTB Club, was officially introduced in the Students Gymkhana with Dr. Padmanabhan Rajan as the faculty advisor. With the long term aim to set up a “Biking Culture” in the Institute and to make IIT Mandi a modal destination for adventure sports’ enthusiastic students, the club set out to a journey. For the following two academic sessions, under its FA’s guidance the club saw a huge growth and recognition. Many motivated faculties and students from all departments (undergraduates and postgraduates) expressed their interests and joined the club, the inventory of the club garage saw various useful additions and the cycling culture in the campus increased. The biggest bottleneck was still the need of personal bikes. To make things work, few existing low end bikes were made available to all the residents of the institute at very cheap rental prices.

The club reached its next milestone on 30th March 2019. The director, Dr. Timothy Anthony Gonsalvis had envisioned IIT Mandi hosting a cycling competition in the 2019. With his and institute’s support and much needed guidance, the club successfully organised a two-day cycling completion in and around the IIT Mandi campus. This event saw a huge participation from students, faculties, staff and even children. Along with our local participation, the event was joined by few prominent riders of the Mandi town as well.

With start of the academic session of 2020, the club was joined by the new faculty advisor Dr. Adarsh Patel. With the support of the faculty advisors, director and the institute administration, the club was able to procure many premium bikes for the student community of IIT Mandi. With these at hand, the club now additionally aims to give each motivated and capable student a chance to experience the thrill of riding in the mountains and motivate everyone to choose a healthier way of life. Frequent Questions

We recommend that you get your own bike. The institute has a few old bicycles, but they are not in very good condition.

There is a Firefox shop in Mandi town. You can hire a vehicle and take your bike to Mandi. Please approach the club for this, in case other bikes too need servicing, and the cost of transporting can be shared.

The shop in Mandi sells only Firefox and Trek, and have limited options. There are many shops in Chandigarh or Delhi. You can buy from there and box the bike and bring it in the overnight bus. Some sellers even ship the bike to Mandi. Many dealers have online purchasing options. But before buying a bike, do try it out first in the showroom. It is always better to buy from a bike shop, than from an all-purpose store. Some shops to explore are: https://www.trackandtrail.in/ https://store.bumsonthesaddle.com/

Bikes are available from Rs 6000 upwards. But of course, the quality of components and the weight of the bike improves with your budget. A decent bike, along with basic accessories will cost you about Rs 20,000.
